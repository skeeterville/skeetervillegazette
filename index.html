🎤 Interview with Chimera Player: Vortex

How did you get into Chimera?
I met a sweet lady by the name of Ila, and she introduced me to the Chimera world. From there, I was hooked.

What's your favorite thing about the Chimeras?
The battles, the versatility, and the breeding concept. Over time, I learned more and more about traits and eyes, and that really pulled me deeper into it.

What's your favorite breed?
It's a dead tie between the Wolf and the Bear.

What's your favorite hide?
Fall Druid is my top favorite. A close second would be Fu Lang Long. Most recent one would be the Oni.

We've seen you made Circuit 27 MVP. Will we see you in Circuit 28?
Absolutely. I'll be doing Circuit 28. Usually with circuits, I have one of the four that performs really well, so I'm looking forward to seeing how this one goes.

A word to the community from Vortex:
For anyone interested in the circuits — especially if you're new — go to the battles and watch. Learn how things work. Bring a friend, even if it's your first time. Give it a try and learn as you go. And don't be afraid to ask questions if you're unsure.

● Classic Comfort
Classic Baked Mac & Cheese
Creamy, baked comfort classic with a golden breadcrumb crust.
Prep: 15 min Cook: 35 min Total: 50 min Serves: 6
Ingredients
  • Elbow Macaroni1½ cups
  • Butter¼ cup
  • All-Purpose Flour2 tbsp
  • Mustard Powder1 tsp
  • Milk2 cups
  • American Cheese8 oz
  • Velveeta8 oz
  • Bread Crumbs¼ cup
Directions
1.Preheat oven to 400°F. Cook macaroni in salted water 6 min until slightly underdone, then drain.
2.Melt butter, stir in flour, mustard, and pepper. Whisk in milk until thick. Add cheeses and stir smooth.
3.Combine with noodles, pour into buttered dish, sprinkle bread crumbs, cover and bake 20–25 min until bubbly.
● Southern
Swamp Gumbo
Warm Up The Belly With Some Great Gumbo — tastes even better the next day!
Ingredients
  • All-purpose flour1 cup
  • Vegetable or canola oil⅔ cup
  • Celery, diced (leaves and all)1 bunch
  • Green bell pepper, diced1
  • Large yellow onion, diced1
  • Green onions, finely chopped1 bunch
  • Fresh parsley, finely chopped1 bunch
  • Garlic cloves2–3
  • Cajun seasoning1–2 tbsp
  • Chicken broth8–10 cups
  • Andouille sausage, sliced into coins12 oz
  • Rotisserie chicken, meat pulled1 whole
  • Shrimp, uncooked or pre-cooked2 cups
  • Hot cooked rice, for servingas needed
Directions
1.Make the Roux: In a large heavy-bottom pot combine flour and oil. Cook on medium-low, stirring constantly 30–45 minutes until dark chocolate color. Don't let it burn.
2.Brown sausage in a skillet on medium-high, 2–3 min per side. Remove to a plate.
3.Deglaze hot skillet with ½ cup broth. Pour broth and drippings into your large soup pot.
4.Add remaining broth, veggies, parsley, garlic, and roux. Stir well.
5.Bring to boil, cook 5–7 min until veggies are slightly tender. Add Cajun seasoning. Add uncooked shrimp and cook 2 more minutes.
6.Add chicken, sausage, and pre-cooked shrimp. Taste and adjust seasoning.
7.Serve warm over rice. Tastes even better the next day!
Submitted by: Roxie Dreamclaw
● Mediterranean
Greek Sheet Pan Chicken
Quick and easy dinner full of flavor
Prep: 8 min Cook: 37 min Total: 45 min Serves: 6
Ingredients
  • — Ladolemono Dressing —
  • Fresh lemon juice1/4 cup
  • Dried oregano1–2 tsp
  • Garlic clove, minced1 large
  • Kosher salt3/4 tsp
  • Black pepper3/4 tsp
  • Extra virgin olive oil3/4 cup
  • — Chicken & Veggies —
  • Red onion, halved & sliced1 large
  • Zucchini, half moons1 medium
  • Orange bell pepper, sliced1 large
  • Tomato, cut into wedges1 large
  • Boneless skinless chicken thighs6–8
  • Pitted Kalamata olives1/4 cup
  • Pitted Castelvetrano olives1/4 cup
  • Feta cheese, cut into chunks4 oz
  • Italian parsley, for garnish1/4 cup
Directions
1.Position a rack in the middle of your oven and heat to 425°F.
2.Make the ladolemono: In a bowl, whisk together lemon juice, oregano, garlic, salt, and pepper. Drizzle in olive oil while whisking until emulsified.
3.Spread onions, zucchini, bell pepper, and tomatoes on a large sheet pan. Season with salt and pepper, pour 1/4 cup sauce over veggies and toss to coat.
4.Season chicken on both sides. Nestle chicken, olives, and feta between the vegetables. Drizzle remaining sauce over everything.
5.Bake on center rack until chicken is cooked through, about 35 minutes. For more color, broil 2 minutes watching closely.
6.Remove from oven, spoon pan juices over chicken, garnish with parsley and serve.
● Mediterranean
Greek Pasta Salad — No Mayo
Fresh, bright and full of flavor — best after a 30 minute chill
Prep: 10 min Cook: 10 min Rest: 30 min Serves: 4
Ingredients
  • — Pasta Salad —
  • Farfalle (bowtie pasta)8 oz
  • English cucumber, chopped1/2
  • Green bell pepper, chopped1
  • Cherry tomatoes, halved10 oz
  • Shallots, chopped1–2
  • Fresh parsley, chopped1 cup
  • Capers, drained & rinsed2 tbsp
  • Pitted Kalamata olives1/2 cup
  • Feta cheese, crumbled1/3 cup
  • — Greek Dressing —
  • Lemon, zested and juiced1
  • Red wine vinegar1 tbsp
  • Garlic cloves, minced2
  • Dried oregano1 tsp
  • Kosher salt & black pepperto taste
  • Extra virgin olive oil1/4 cup
Directions
1.Cook the pasta in salted boiling water about 8 minutes. Drain and set aside to cool slightly.
2.Make the dressing: In a large bowl mix lemon juice and zest, red wine vinegar, garlic, oregano, salt and pepper. Whisk together, then drizzle in the olive oil while whisking.
3.Add the pasta, cucumber, bell pepper, tomatoes, shallots, parsley, capers, and olives to the bowl. Toss to coat.
4.Add the feta and toss again very gently.
5.Cover and refrigerate at least 30 minutes before serving — up to 24 hours for best flavor. Give a final toss and serve cold or at room temperature.

§ About Us — The Team Behind The Gazette

Meet The Team

The husband and wife duo bringing you Skeeterville's finest news every Sunday
The Skeeterville Gazette • Est. 2026
Isac Dreamclaw
Editor-in-Chief • Co-Founder

Thank you for picking up the Skeeterville Gazette. Every great town has a story. Ours begins not just in a place, but in a promise. I first stepped into the world of Second Life in 2007, never imagining that behind a screen and across the miles, destiny was waiting. In 2014, I met a remarkable woman named Roxie — a light I didn't know I was searching for. I was rooted on the East Coast; she was shining on the West. What began as conversations turned into connection, and connection into something neither distance nor time could dim. After months of building a love through late nights and shared dreams, I boarded a plane and flew toward what my heart already knew — my soulmate, my forever flame. Fourteen years later, that same love still guides us. Together, we built more than a partnership — we built a home. What started as sketches and scribbled ideas became a living, breathing bayou when we claimed a sim in Second Life and created Skeeterville Juke Joint — a place where music plays, laughter echoes, and community thrives. The idea for this Gazette was Roxie's — born from her vision of a town connected not just by land, but by story. She imagined a paper our Chimera family could read to discover new destinations, celebrate one another, and strengthen the bonds that make Skeeterville feel like home. This Gazette is more than ink on a page — it is a reflection of love, creativity, and the community we are honored to share with you. Welcome to the story. Welcome to Skeeterville.

Roxie Dreamclaw
Editor-in-Chief • Co-Founder

My name is Roxie, Chief and Co-Editor of the Skeeterville Gazette, alongside my husband, Isac. I landed in Second Life in 2011 and was immediately amazed by all it had to offer — not just the creativity and endless possibilities, but the friendships waiting to be formed. It was here that I met my husband — my best friend, my heart and soul, my soulmate. As we journeyed together through Second Life, I discovered Chimeras. My very first Chimera was Mail Order Bride, and I was fascinated by everything you could do with a breedable in Second Life. The creativity, the care, the community — it opened up a whole new world for me. From that shared excitement, Isac and I came up with an idea: to build Skeeterville Juke Joint — a place where the Chimera community could gather, share laughs and stories, and create exciting memories together with their Chimeras. What began as a simple idea grew into a welcoming bayou home filled with music, friendship, and fun. And now, through this Gazette, we continue building that sense of community — one story at a time. — Roxie Chief & Co-Editor
